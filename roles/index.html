My Roles and Skills

I work on and around the Shop Floor in a convenience format. Our store is very busy, being in a key location in Old Town, making this role extremely fast-paced at key times. As part of a small team, we consistently deliver profits in excess of our store budget.

  • Managing self-scan checkouts, including age-approval and weight mismatches
  • Serving customers with larger baskets and trolleys
  • Serving customers paying with cash or those who wish to be served by a person
  • Handling cash safely and without error; avoiding short-change and ringing the change scams etcetera
  • Playing a key part in loss-prevention, both in the detection and confrontation of shoplifters
  • Serving customers with cards that need to be swiped such as AMEX, Visa/Mastercard Credit, Debit & Electron; other cards from overseas banks
  • Performing refunds with cash and cards, Clubcard points and prices transactions while maintainging company policy as it pertains to these transactions
  • Handling newspaper vouchers, gift cards and cash vouchers
  • Serving customers spirits & champagne; tobacco products, e-cigarettes, nicotine pouches et al.
  • Serving National Lottery customers wishing to purchase or claim prizes pertaining to scratchcards and draw-based tickets
  • Using other National Lottery Terminal functions such as Reports & Invoice, Cancellations et alii
  • Asking customers for valid proof-of-age, withstanding our Think-25 policy
  • Managing Price Integrity (see below) during quieter times
  • Filling Beers, Wines & Spirits; tobacco products et alii during quieter times
  • Facing Beers, Wines & Spirits and Large Sweets
  • Performing till closing procedures including: face-up of Large Sweets and Beers, Wines & Spirits; couting scratchcards and entering those totals using the relevant till & till function and locking the dispenser; printing draw-based and scratchcard reports from the lottery machine and signing-out; newspaper returns using PDAs; posting notes for CIT Pickup; taring balances in the cash drawer (Till Reset); weighing cash in the till (Till Check); locking the till; turning off screens to conserve power and life of assets; closing tobacco shutter and any final put-backs; closing fridge doors & refrigeration blinds to conserve energy and perishable product life.
  • Filling hundreds of lines of Beers, Wines & Spirits including chilled wine, ready-to-drink cans, cider and perry et alii
  • Following Restricted Fill; using RF tags on high-value and high-risk products for loss-prevention and as a deterrent
  • Acting in a supportive role for the colleague operating the CSD due to the proximity of the alcohol aisle
